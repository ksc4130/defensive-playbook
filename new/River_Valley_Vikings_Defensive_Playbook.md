## 1. DEFENSIVE PHILOSOPHY & IDENTITY

> "We're a pressure defense. We attack with plus-one advantages and play zero behind it. When we're not pressuring, we play man. If they pick it up, we change the picture and come again. We don't sit back and wait for offenses to beat us. We attack. We set edges, we own our gaps, and we get 11 hats to the ball. No explosives. No free yards. Make them earn every first down, and when they don't, take the ball. **From snap to whistle, we play with violent intentions.**"

### Base Structure

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Personnel** | 4-2-5 base; 3-4 / 3-2-6 packages |
| **Strength** | Field ("protect space") |
| **Shell** | Two-high |
| **Box** | 6-man |
| **Call Format** | Front + Stunt + Blitz tag(s) + Coverage/Tags |
| **Communication** | Signaled front/stunt/blitz; coverage is verbal. Safeties echo and relay. |
| **Coverage Identity** | Zeus and Zorro are our identity. NINJA is the engine. Viking is the safety net. |

### Non-Negotiables

1. **No explosives.** Make them snap it again. Eliminate busts and cheap yards.
2. **Aggressive with discipline.** Attack without losing leverage or committing penalties.
3. **Box the run.** Set hard edges, compress gaps, keep the ball in help.
4. **Relentless pursuit.** 11 to the ball, correct angles, swarming tackles — finish every snap.
5. **Win situations.** 1st down sets the series; 3rd down = get off the field.
6. **Smart aggression.** Plus-one rushers with zero behind it. No-win for the offense. Call off the dawgs when needed.
7. **Communication standard.** Simple, loud, early. Everyone echoes. Alerts: BUMP / BANJO / EXCHANGE / UNDER.

### The Standard
The baseline. Every snap, every rep, every day.
- No busted coverages — know your call, know your rules, communicate
- Stay in phase — maintain leverage in man; no trailing, no freelancing
- No free routes — collision every release; reroute, disrupt timing, deny easy access
- No lost edges — contain player keeps QB and ball inside
- No uncovered gaps — know your fit with stunts/pressures
- No missed tackles — leverage + near foot + wrap; eliminate YAC
- No penalties that extend drives
- No loafs — pursuit is mandatory

### Key Definitions

- **Contain:** Maintain outside leverage. Squeeze the run inside. Do not let anything get outside you. Set the edge.
- **Force:** Collision at or behind the LOS. Turn the ball inside. More aggressive than contain — attack the ball carrier.

## 3. 4-DOWN FRONT CATALOG

### Global Rules (no stunts)

- M fits the open gap to the field side
- W fits the open gap to the boundary side
- Open gap logic: 3-tech closes B → open A; 2i/1 closes A → open B
- A and E = contain (set edge, no wrong-arm)
- B = field force. D = boundary force
- 2-back (20/21 personnel): 6-man box default; B is first insert if adding 7th hat
- Puller rule (Power/Counter/Wing-T): Box + overlap; edge outside, M/W inside-out off pull path
- QB run (CAMP): DE (A) is QB player

### TE SET — Default 4-Down Adjustment

Overrides **ALL** fronts **EXCEPT GRIZZLY** when TE/Y-off surface.

- Set front to TE: 3-tech to TE, 2i away
- B inserts into box at LB depth field side, responsible for C(F) gap if the TE to the field or B(F) gap if the TE is to the boundary
- End to TE aligns 7-tech and plays contain
- *Example (TE to field, SHADE):* A goes to 7-tech on TE, T goes to 3-tech (to TE), N goes to 2i (away from TE), E stays 5 boundary. B inserts at LB depth. **Gap fits: B = C gap, M = A gap, W = B gap.**
- *Example (TE to boundary, SHADE):* E goes to 7-tech on TE, N goes to 3-tech (to TE), T goes to 2i (away from TE), A stays 5 field. B inserts at LB depth. **Gap fits: B = B gap, M = A gap, W = C gap. SHADE checks to UNDER** (front shifts to boundary TE).
- **TE SET triggers on alignment, not personnel.** If the TE splits out as a WR (more than 3 yards from nearest OL), treat him as a receiver — no TE SET.

### Front Alignments

| Front | A | T | N | E | M Fit | W Fit |
|-------|---|---|---|---|-------|-------|
| **SHADE** (set field) | 5 | 3 | 2i | 5 | Open A (field) | Open B (boundary) |
| **UNDER** (set boundary) | 5 | 2i | 3 | 5 | Open B (field) | Open A (boundary) |
| **EYES** (balanced) | 5 | 2i | 2i | 5 | Open B | Open B |
| **WIDE** (balanced) | 5 | 3 | 3 | 5 | Open A | Open A |
| **DEUCES** (balanced) | 5 | 2 | 2 | 5 | React to T (T closes A → M fills B; T stays B → M fills A) | React to N (same logic) |
| **GRIZZLY** (balanced) | 4i | 2i | 2i | 4i | Head up center at LB depth (no TE); head up guard to TE side at LB depth (TE present); C gap | OLB |

| **BOSS UNDER** (bigs bnd) | 5 | 1(B) | 3(B) | 5 | A gap (field) | B gap (field) |

## 4. 3-DOWN PACKAGES (MINT / ACE / JET / SLIP)

### Common Rules

- Field OLB = B. Boundary OLB = E.
- T/N may swap by personnel.
- Slice rule: backside OLB has slicer.
- Strength = Field.

### ILB Fits (Locked)

- **Play-to-field:** M = stack B-C gap field, inside-out. W slow-plays A gap (patient shuffle, hunt cutback), inside-out.
- **Play-away (from strength):** M slow-plays A gap (patient shuffle, hunt cutback). W stack B-C gap boundary.
- **Front can affect these**

### MINT (4i / 0 / 4i)

- **Signal:** mimic tighting a jar
- A=4i, T=0, N=4i. B/E = OLBs.
- Contain: Man Zeus = B. NINJA/VIKING (MINT only) = M.
- **MINT + zone contain technique:** On zone read, M scrapes over the top to the C gap. If A (at 4i) gets washed inside, M becomes the edge player. M does NOT sit in his gap — he must get outside A's alignment on QB keep.

### ACE (4 / 0 / 4)

- **Signal:** 1 finger up
- A=4, T=0, N=4. B/E = OLBs.
- A, T, N **ALL** 2-gapping (no stunt). Contains = OLBs (B and E).

### JET (5 / 0 / 5)

- **Signal:** Mimic airplane with arms
- A=5, T=0, N=5. B/E = OLBs.
- T is 2-gapping. A and N are contain (C-gap edges).

### SLIP (5 / 0 / 4i)

- **Signal:** Mime pushing a wall to the side (two hands)
- A=5, T=0, N=4i. B/E = OLBs.
- 1-gap penetrating. A and E are contain.
- T at 0 goes to the slip-side A gap, except when stunts are on.
- **Stunts are legal with SLIP.** 
- **ILB gap fits:** M = B gap field. W = A gap boundary.
- **Use:** 3-down vs spread with NINJA or VIKING behind it. Takes B out of conflict — B plays coverage clean without force/contain responsibilities.

### Contain Matrix

| Package + Coverage | Contain |
|--------------------|---------|
| MINT + Zeus | B+E |
| MINT + NINJA/VIKING | M+E |
| ACE + Zeus | B+E |
| ACE + NINJA/VIKING | A/M+N/E |
| JET  | A+N |
| SLIP + Zeus | A and E (B plays coverage clean) |
| SLIP + NINJA/VIKING | A and E (B plays coverage clean) |

### CAMP QB Player (Universal Rule)

**Whoever has contain has the QB.**

This one rule works across all packages and coverages:
- 4-down base: A has contain → A has QB.
- Mint/Ace (A at 4i/4): B has contain → B has QB.
- Jet/Slip (A at 5): A has contain → A has QB.
- MINT + NINJA/VIKING: M has contain → M has QB.
- CRASH: B has field contain → B has field QB. W has boundary contain → W has boundary QB.
- CRASH + Zorro: M has field contain → M has field QB. B has RB. W has boundary contain → W has boundary QB.

### TE Adjustment (3-Down)

OLB-only adjust: bump B/E alignments to TE/Y-off surface. Keep Mint/Ace/Jet/Slip interior intact.

## 5. STUNT CATALOG

DL coaching point: read run/pass; on run read get heel depth and find  the ball, on pass maintain rush lanes (collapse the pocket). Edges play normal unless noted.

### SLANT (to field)

- 4-down: T+N slant to field. 3-down: A+T+N slant to the field.
- **NOT** with Shade. Best fronts: Under, BOSS Under.
- Best pressures: Mike, Will, Bandit, sWarM, BooM, BoW.

### ANGLE (to boundary)

- Same as Slant but to boundary.
- **NOT** with Under/BOSS Under. Best fronts: Shade, BOSS.

### PINCH

- 4-down: T+N pinch A gaps (if already in A, play base). 3-down: A+N pinch B gaps.
- A and E normal. 
- **NOT** with eyes or grizzly. Best fronts: Shade, Under, Wide, Deuces.

### JACKS

- 4-down: T+N shoot B gaps. 3-down: A+N expand to C gaps (NOT pinch).
- A and E contain. 
- **NOT** with Wide. Best fronts: Eyes, Shade, Under. Best pressure: sWarM.

### SPLIT

- With BOSS: 1-tech crosses Center's face to opposite A gap. With BOSS UNDER: same concept, boundary side. With GRIZZLY: field side A and T stunt out one gap.
- A and E contain. Looks weird with BOSS but plays like Shade. Best: Mike, sWarM.
- **SPLIT is only legal with BOSS, BOSS UNDER, and GRIZZLY.** Not called with other fronts.

### ANCHOR ATTACK

- A attacks OL, washes them inside (closes one gap in). **NOT** paired with Cobra.
- **Best fronts:** Under, Eyes, Boss Under, Shade (with Pinch or Angle). Principle: A needs an open gap inside him to attack into.
- Hammer (Anchor Attack + B edge blitz) is a **go stunt** — execute regardless of run/pass read.

### EDGE ATTACK

- E attacks OL, closes one gap inside. **CAN** pair with Cobra.
- **Best fronts:** Shade, Eyes, Boss, Under (with Pinch or Slant). Principle: E needs an open gap inside him to attack into.
- Shave (Edge Attack + W edge blitz) is a **go stunt** — execute regardless of run/pass read.

### CRASH

- A+E shoot B gaps. T+N shoot A gaps. **B = field contain** (replaces A). **W = boundary contain** (replaces E). **M = free LB** (free dender unless TE).
- **With TE/Y-off/H:** M takes the gap they create (e.g., C gap to the TE).
- Best paired with **Zorro** (B and M switch alignments — B aligns ~8 yds middle to key RB on pass, M moves to field force/contain area. This allows B to have RB on pass while M handles the extra gap from the TE).
- **CRASH + Zorro CAMP exception:** M takes field contain (and therefore the QB per CAMP). B keys the RB from ~8 yds middle. W has boundary contain. This is the only CRASH pairing where B does NOT have field contain.
- **NOT** with Grizzly or Freebird. Best fronts: Wide, Shade, Under. Best pressure: BoW.

### ANCHOR RAVEN (A 5→B)

- A shoots B gap from 5-tech. Signal: swing anchor flap elbow.
- Not with Hammer or staB. If + Bandit = BANDIT RAVEN.

### EDGE RAVEN (E 5→B)

- E shoots B gap from 5-tech. Signal: chop hands flap elbow.

### VEER (Slant to Motion / Slant to TE)

- 4-down: T+N slant. 3-down: A+T+N slant.
- Two variants: (1) DL slants toward the motion, (2) DL slants toward the TE.
- Reactive stunt — direction determined by offensive alignment/motion.

## 6. BLITZ / PRESSURE TAGS

**Rule:** All blitzes hit your gap responsibility unless noted.

**Pre-snap discipline:** Blitzing players DO NOT creep pre-snap. Hold base alignment until the snap. Timing of the blitz is post-snap only. No exceptions.

### Single Add-Ons

| Call | Who Blitzes |
|------|-------------|
| **Mike** | M blitzes his gap |
| **Will** | W blitzes his gap |
| **Bandit** | B blitzes his gap |
| **staB** | B blitzes one gap inside EMOL normally C or B gap |
| **Dawg** | D blitzes his gap |

### Special Tags

- **staB** — B one gap inside EOL (normally B or C gap)
- **Freebird** — Cover 1 only. The post safety (per variant) blitzes A gap: Oregon = FS blitzes, Oklahoma = D blitzes, Ohio = B blitzes. Remaining defenders cover per CAMP distribution minus the blitzing player.
- **Cobra** — Boundary corner blitz. **Cover 0 only, paired with Zunnel. Hash-only call** (ball must be on the hash. Pre-snap: standard zorro shell. At the snap: BC blitzes (6th rusher, replaces B in Zunnel). D rotates down to #1 boundary. B takes #2 boundary (from Zorro-depth pre-snap alignment, ~8 yds middle). FS takes #2 field. M/W funnel RB (Zunnel rules). **Cobra requires B in coverage — cannot pair with any pressure that sends B (Bandit, BooM, BoW, Eat, Hammer, staB).**

### Combination Pressures

| Call | Who Blitzes |
|------|-------------|
| **sWarM** | M + W |
| **BooM** | B + M |
| **BoW** | B + W |
| **MaD** | M + D |
| **Eat** | M + W + B (rush peel) |

### Packaged Pressures (Married to Stunts)

- **Hammer** — B edge blitz + Anchor Attack (A washes OL inside, creating edge for B)
- **Shave** — W edge blitz + Edge Attack (E attacks OL)
- **Bandit Raven** — B blitzes C gap, A shoots B gap
- **Will Raven** — W blitzes C gap, E shoots B gap

### Weekly Called Pressures (Locked Friday Menu)

Mike, Will, Bandit, sWarM, BooM, BoW, Hammer, Eat.
Zeus/Z-family featured separately.

### MUG (Alignment Tag)

M and/or W walk up to the LOS at their pre-snap gap assignment. Responsibilities do not change — same gap fit, same coverage drop, same read keys. Mug changes the pre-snap picture only.

- **MUG** — both M and W walk up to their gap.
- **M MUG** — only M walks up.
- **W MUG** — only W walks up.
- **E Mug** — 3 down edge walks up

**Pre-snap gap is front-dependent:**

| Front | M Walks To | W Walks To |
|-------|-----------|-----------|
| SHADE | A gap (field) | B gap (boundary) |
| UNDER | B gap (field) | A gap (boundary) |
| EYES | B gap | B gap |
| WIDE | A gap | A gap |

**Coaching points:**
- Coverage drops start late — M/W must get to their zone/assignment quickly after the snap. In NINJA, M still owns hook/crosser; in CLAMP, W still owns hook/curl #3 push.
- In Zeus, Mug is natural — M is already an add-on rusher on pass read. Walking up just gets him there faster.
- Do NOT tip blitz vs Mug. Mug players hold the LOS alignment and play their base assignment. Blitzing players also hold alignment and hit their gap. The offense should not be able to tell the difference pre-snap.

### Game-Plan Specials (Not Weekly — Installed as Needed)

Dawg, MaD, Shave, Freebird, Cobra These are available in the playbook but only installed for specific game plans. Not part of the weekly menu.

### Freebird + Cover 1 Definition

Freebird is a **Cover 1 only** pressure. The post safety (per variant) blitzes as the extra rusher to the open A gap. Wehn freebird is called we are effectively in cover 0

- **Freebird + Oregon (FS blitzes):** FS is the post → FS rushes. Remaining coverage: D has #2 boundary (or #3 away), B has #2 field (or #3 away), FC/BC have #1, M/W funnel RB. No post safety — accept the risk.
- **Freebird + Oklahoma (D blitzes):** D is the post → D rushes. FS has #2 boundary (already on boundary side from pre-snap switch), B has #2 field, FC/BC have #1, M/W funnel RB. No post safety — accept the risk.
- **Freebird + Ohio (B blitzes):** B is the post → B rushes from ~8 yds middle (looks like a blitz from depth). FS has #2 field, D has #2 boundary, FC/BC have #1, M/W funnel RB. No post safety — accept the risk.

## 7. COVERAGE: NINJA (COVER 7 FAMILY)

Coach calls "NINJA." DBs auto-check. FS owns field checks. D owns boundary checks. Safeties are fixers.

### Alignments

- **Corners (FC/BC):** Inside leverage on #1, 6 yards on #1.
- **Safeties (FS/D):** 10-12 yds deep. 1 yd inside #2. No #2: poach/post posture, in a position to be force away from trips and drop MOF. Compressed/stack: tighten 1-2 steps, ready to BANJO ( match the new #2).
- **B (Bandit):** Apex to field, split between #2 and the box.

### NINJA vs 2x2 — MOD / CLAMP

Two techniques that can be called to **either side.** Default: MOD to field, CLAMP to boundary. CLAMP can also be run to the field. MOD can also be run to the boundary, especially when the ball is in the **middle of the field**.

#### MOD (Sky Principles)

Three-man triangle: Corner + Safety + Flat Player. Corner keys #1. Safety reads #2.

| Position | Key | Rule |
|----------|-----|------|
| **Corner** | #1, reads to #2 | Match #1-to-#2. Stay on top of #1 deep/outside. If #1 breaks short → pass off, zone off looking for corner from #2 or to rob #2. |
| **Safety** | #2, reads to #1 | Match #2-to-#1. #2 vertical → match #2. #2 out/under → work to high hole (check for backside crosser) or rob #1. |
| **Flat Player** | #2, expands to flat | Cover down on #2. Match #2. Push (carry vertical). First man to flat. If flat clears → relate back to hook zone. |

**Default field MOD:** FC = corner, FS = safety, B = flat player.
**Boundary MOD:** BC = corner, D = safety, W = flat player.
 

Corner and safety **both read #2.** Clamp viability is a **pre-snap check based on receiver splits** — if #2 is aligned within 5-7 yards of #1 (tight/compressed), CLAMP works. If #2 is split wider, default to MOD. This is on the safety to decide if he can get over top of #1's fade (deep) if #2 goes out.

| Position | Key | Rule |
|----------|-----|------|
| **Corner** | #2 | #2 out (safety calls clamp) → **jump #2**, take him in the flat (take the wheel don't sit). #2 vertical → stay on #1. #2 inside → stay on #1. |
| **Safety** | #2, reads to #2 | #2 vertical → match #2. #2 out → **flip to #1 deep** (replace corner). #2 inside → carry or pass off #2, bracket #1. |
| **Hook/Curl (#3)** | Late push, reads #2 first | Late push drop to hook/curl. Takes whoever becomes #3. |

**Default boundary CLAMP:** BC = corner, D = safety, W = hook/curl (#3).
**Field CLAMP:** FC = corner, FS = safety, B = hook/curl (#3).

#### M and W Rules

**M — Primary crosser carrier (all configurations):**

| Read | Rule |
|------|------|
| **Base** | Hook zone. Eyes to QB, relate to underneath threats. |
| **First crosser enters his zone** | Wall it, carry it. Default is not to hand it off. Yell cross if Will echos they will hand the routes off to each over, think mesh. |
| **Crosser carries to boundary** | M carries all the way through. Calls **"CROSS!"** so W knows to stay on his assignment. |
| **Mesh (two crossers)** | Take the deeper / more dangerous crosser. Flat player (B) relates back to pick up the shallow crosser. (see above for crossers, where the Will echos **"CROSS!"** call) |

**W — CLAMP hook/curl #3 (default configuration):**

| Read | Rule |
|------|------|
| **Base** | Late push drop to hook/curl. Read #2 trigger, then settle for #3. |
| **Hears "CROSS!" from M** | Stay on #3. M has the crosser. Do not chase. |
| **No #3 threat and crosser enters his zone** | Relate to the crosser. |

**W — MOD flat player (boundary MOD configuration):**

| Read | Rule |
|------|------|
| **Base** | Push to flat. Widen alignment (if possible cover down #2). First man to flat. |
| **Flat is clear** | Relate back to hook zone. |
| **Matchg the wheel** | if the flat route is a wheel carry it |

#### Crosser Handling Summary

| Situation | M | W | B |
|-----------|---|---|---|
| **No crossers** | Hook zone, relate to threats | CLAMP: hook/curl #3 | MOD: flat player |
| **Single crosser (field → bnd)** | Carries crosser all the way through, calls "CROSS!" | Stays on #3 | Flat clears → relates back to hook |
| **Single crosser (bnd → field)** | Picks up crosser heading field | Stays on #3 | Stays in flat |
| **Mesh (two crossers)** | Takes deeper crosser, should get echo from Will for swtch | Takes #3 (or shallow crosser if #3 not threatening) | Relates back to pick up shallow crosser |

#### Default NINJA 2x2 Alignment

| Side | Technique | Corner | Safety | B / W | M |
|------|-----------|--------|--------|-------|---|
| **Field** | MOD | FC (keys #1) | FS (reads #2-to-#1) | B = flat player | Hook / crosser carrier |
| **Boundary** | CLAMP | BC (keys #2) | D (reads #2-to-#1) | W = hook/curl (#3) | Hook / crosser carrier |

#### When to Flip

| Flip | When | Corner | Safety | B / W | M |
|------|------|--------|--------|-------|---|
| **CLAMP to field** | Field #2 tight split to #1 (5-7 yds or less) | FC (jumps #2 out) | FS (#1 deep if #2 out) | B → hook/curl (#3) | Hook / crosser carrier |
| **MOD to boundary** | Ball in MOF and #2 has wide splits to #1 | BC (keys #1 deep) | D (reads #2-to-#1) | W → flat player | Hook / crosser carrier |

**Safety to mod side can work to high if we get a lot of high crossing routes**

### NINJA vs 3x1 — POACH

- **Trips to field: D is the poach safety.**
- **Trips to boundary: FS is the poach safety.**
- #3 vertical: poach safety takes it (no free seam).
- #3 shallow/under: poach player is essentially the MOF safety looking to poach the post.
- Backside stays sound — no free go balls or glance RPO.
- Non poach safety slides post-snap to **10-12 yards deep MOF, splitting #2 and #3 on the trips side.** Reads #3 first. #3 vertical = match immediately. #3 underneath = settle at depth, eyes to QB, overlap crossers. Do not drift past the far hash — stay in position to break on throws. Pre-snap he is still at 10-12 over #2 in his normal spot.
- **Corner away from the trip is MES (Man except shallow)** if is under give **Under!** call and him carry looking for the flat (potentional) wheel by the RB. If the RB is out flat pass the the under and jump the flat top shoulder. If the RB is not out Flat carry the under route near the box and pass off looking for cross from the trips side, if there not a cross settle with depth.

## 11. PRE-SNAP DISGUISE & ALIGNMENT RULES 2 primary shells

### Core Principle

**Every coverage we run looks the same before the snap.** The offense sees the same two-high shell every time. DBs execute their coverage responsibilities post-snap.

> The offense should never know what coverage we are in until after the ball is snapped.

### Exceptions (2)

1. **OHIO:** B moves from apex to ~8 yards deep in the middle of the formation to become the post/MOF safety. His movement tips the coverage.
2. **ZORRO:** B moves from apex to ~8 yards deep in the middle of the formation to key the RB. His alignment change tips the coverage.
3. **Zunnel with cobra call:** B moves from apex to ~8 yards deep in the middle of the formation to key the RB. His alignment change tips the coverage.
4. **Trips with man:** trips with man will change the picture. We can look at show man. This will evolve depending on our personnel.

### Universal Pre-Snap Alignment

| Position | Pre-Snap Alignment | Key |
|----------|-------------------|-----|
| FC / BC | Inside leverage (half man, outside foot split the receiver), 6 yards on #1 | Do not tip coverage with depth or leverage changes |
| FS | 10-12 yds deep, inside leverage on #2 (~1 yd inside #2) | If no #2: hash-to-middle, eyes on #3/QB |
| D | 10-12 yds deep, inside leverage on #2 (~1 yd inside #2) | If no #2: hash-to-middle, eyes on #3/QB |
| B | Apex to field, split between #2 and box | Same spot every coverage (aside from above exceptions) |
| M / W | Standard LB depth (4-5 yds) over assigned gap | Run-fit alignment does not change by coverage |

### Formation-Driven Adjustments (NOT Coverage-Driven)

- **Compressed/stack splits:** Safeties tighten 1-2 yds, ready for BANJO. This is a man adjustment.
- **Motion:** NINJA re-checks (POACH/MOD/CLAMP). Man coverages use BUMP-BUMP. Either way, the pre-snap shell resets the same.

## 14. PASS CONCEPT BREAKDOWN BY COVERAGE

How each coverage defends common pass concepts. Use this section to game-plan coverage calls based on opponent tendencies.

### 14.1 FOUR VERTICALS

All four eligible receivers push vertical. Stretches the defense deep, looking for the seam between zones or a 1-on-1 matchup.

#### vs NINJA (Cover 7)
- **FC:** Matches #1 vertical — stay on top, deny explosive.
- **FS:** Top-down on #2 seam. Already in position — no rotation needed.
- **D:** Controls #2 boundary seam. Already in position.
- **BC:** Clamps #1 vertical.
- **B:** Carries #2/#3 vertical threat until FS takes over, then looks for crosser.
- **M/W:** Hook — look for RB leaking into seam.
- **KEY:** Two-high caps all 4 seams. Seams are bracketed. **Best zone look vs 4 verts.**

#### vs OREGON (Cover 1, Post = FS)
- **FC/BC:** Man #1 vertical — stay on top, don't get beat deep.
- **FS:** Post safety → rotates to MOF. Helps whichever side #2 wins on the seam.
- **D:** Man #2 boundary — carry vertical, lean on FS help over the top.
- **B:** Man #2 field — carry vertical, lean on FS help.
- **M/W:** RB funnel.
- **KEY:** Four seams stress the single post. FS has to choose a side.

#### vs OKLAHOMA (Cover 1, Post = D)
- **D:** Starts field pre-snap → rotates to MOF post. Helps on seams.
- **FS:** Starts boundary pre-snap → man #2 boundary seam.
- **B:** Man #2 field seam.
- **FC/BC:** Man #1 vertical.
- **M/W:** RB funnel.
- **KEY:** Same vulnerability as Oregon — single post vs 4 seams.

#### vs ZEUS (Cover 0)
- **FC/BC:** Man #1. Win at the line or lose — no help.
- **FS:** Man #2 field seam. **D:** Man #2 boundary seam. Both carrying vertical.
- **M:** Primary add-on rusher. **B/W:** RB funnel.
- **KEY:** No post safety. If any receiver wins, it's a touchdown. DL must get pressure.

#### vs VIKING (Cover 3)
- **FC/BC:** Bail to deep 1/3. Stay on top of #1 vertical in their zone.
- **FS:** Deep middle 1/3. Splits the seams. Best single-high look vs 4 verts.
- **D:** Seam-curl-flat. Walls #2 boundary vertical, doesn't carry deep.
- **B:** Curl/flat. Walls #2 field vertical, passes off to FC deep.
- **KEY:** Three deep zones cap all four verticals. FS splits both seams in middle 1/3.

### 14.2 MESH / CROSSERS

Two receivers cross underneath at 5-8 yards (mesh) or 10-15 yards (drive). Creates pick/rub action and tests zone defenders' ability to pass off.

#### vs NINJA (Cover 7)
- **FC/BC:** Pass off crosser as it leaves zone.
- **FS:** Top-down on #2 vertical.
- **D:** Controls #2 vertical. Walls crosser entering his zone.
- **B:** Walls crosser entering field zone.
- **M/W:** Hook zone — sit in the wash of crossers, collision and disrupt timing.
- **KEY:** Zone pass-offs handle picks naturally. No defender gets screened. **Best look vs mesh.**

#### vs OREGON / OKLAHOMA (Cover 1)
- **FC/BC:** Man on #1 — must fight through the mesh/pick. BANJO or SWITCH helps.
- **Post safety (FS or D):** Reads QB eyes and jumps the crosser QB targets.
- **B:** Man #2 field vertical.
- **D/FS (non-post):** Man #2 boundary vertical.
- **M/W:** If RB releases, take him. Otherwise become RAT.
- **KEY:** Picks/rubs give man defenders trouble. Need aggressive collision at the LOS.

#### vs ZEUS (Cover 0)
- **FC/BC:** Man through the mesh. Must fight picks with no help.
- **FS:** Man #2 field vertical. **D:** Man #2 boundary vertical.
- **M:** Rush. DL pressure must force a quick throw.
- **KEY:** Most vulnerable coverage vs mesh/picks. Need instant pressure.

#### vs VIKING (Cover 3)
- **FC/BC:** Bail deep 1/3. Prevents shots over the top after mesh bait.
- **D/B:** Wall crossers coming through their zone. Don't chase — let them come to you.
- **M/W:** Collision in the hook zone, disrupt timing.
- **KEY:** Zone handles picks. Three deep prevents shots over mesh bait.

### 14.3 SMASH (Corner / Hitch)

\#1 runs a hitch at 5-6 yards. #2 runs a corner route behind it. Attacks the flat defender — does he sit on the hitch or carry the corner?

#### vs NINJA (Cover 7)
- **FC:** Matches #1 hitch — sits on it.
- **FS:** Already over #2 — corner route runs right into his coverage. Stays on top.
- **BC:** Clamps #1 hitch.
- **D:** Controls #2 boundary. If corner, carries it.
- **B:** Reads #2 out. If corner, falls under it (hitch help).
- **KEY:** FS already over #2 — corner runs into his coverage. **Strong matchup.**

#### vs OREGON / OKLAHOMA (Cover 1)
- **FC/BC:** Man on #1 hitch — easy, just sit on it.
- **B/D (whoever has #2):** Carries corner route man-to-man.
- **Post safety:** Helps on corner if it goes toward MOF.
- **KEY:** Man handles smash well. Post safety can help on corner.

#### vs ZEUS (Cover 0)
- Man on #1 = hitch is easy. Man on #2 = must carry corner with no help.
- **KEY:** If #2 runs a great corner route, no help over the top.

#### vs VIKING (Cover 3)
- **FC/BC:** Bail to deep 1/3. Corner route runs into their deep zone — stays on top.
- **B/D:** Curl-flat / seam-curl-flat. Sits on the hitch, passes corner to deep 1/3.
- **KEY:** Deep 1/3 caps corner, flat covers hitch. **Best zone look vs smash.**

### 14.4 FLOOD / SAIL (3-Level Stretch)

Three receivers to one side at three levels: deep (corner/go), intermediate (dig/out), short (flat/arrow). Stretches two defenders into three zones.

#### vs NINJA (Cover 7)
- **FS:** Takes deepest threat (corner/go) on MOD side.
- **B:** Walls the intermediate route.
- **FC:** Matches #1 if he's running the flat.
- **M:** May need to trigger to the flat if B is occupied.
- **KEY:** The third level (flat) can stress B if he's slow to react.

#### vs OREGON / OKLAHOMA (Cover 1)
- Man assignments handle the levels. Each defender carries his man.
- Post safety leans toward the flood side to help on the deepest route.
- **KEY:** If flood comes from trips, post must commit to one side.

#### vs ZEUS (Cover 0)
- Man coverage — each defender runs with his guy. No flat conflict.
- **KEY:** No deep help on the corner/go route.

#### vs VIKING (Cover 3)
- Deep 1/3 defender caps the corner/go route.
- B or D handles the intermediate. M/W one of them triggers to flat.
- **KEY:** Flat can be vulnerable if underneath dropper is slow or out-leveraged.

### 14.5 LEVELS (Hi-Lo Crossers)

Two crossers at different depths (drag at 5 yds, dig at 12 yds). Reads the hook/curl defender — does he drop deep or sit shallow?

#### vs NINJA (Cover 7)
- **FS/D:** Relate to deeper crosser (dig) entering their zone.
- **B/M/W:** Wall the shallow crosser (drag). Don't chase deep.
- **KEY:** Zone reads hi-lo. Defenders play in between. No picks. **Strong matchup.**

#### vs OREGON / OKLAHOMA (Cover 1)
- Man defenders carry their assigned receiver across the field — long chase.
- Post safety reads QB eyes and helps on the deeper crosser.
- **KEY:** Crossing routes create long chases for man defenders.

#### vs ZEUS (Cover 0)
- Man on each crosser with no help. Must stay attached through the route.
- **KEY:** Long crosses + no help = vulnerability if defender trails.

#### vs VIKING (Cover 3)
- **D/B:** Seam-curl-flat — wall the deeper crosser as it enters zone.
- **M/W:** Hook — collision the shallow crosser, disrupt timing.
- **KEY:** Zone layers naturally handle hi-lo. Crossers run into drops.

### 14.6 CURL / FLAT

\#1 runs a curl at 12-14 yards. #2 runs a flat/out at 5 yards. Classic 2-man read of the flat defender.

#### vs NINJA (Cover 7)
- **FC:** Sits on curl. **FS:** Over the top of #2, flat route runs into his zone.
- **BC:** Clamps curl. **D:** Controls #2 flat.
- **B:** Reads #2 out to flat, walls it. If vertical, carries.
- **KEY:** Two-high = both curl and flat are covered. No conflict for flat defender.

#### vs OREGON / OKLAHOMA (Cover 1)
- **CB:** Man on #1 curl — sit on it. **B/D:** Man on #2 flat — run with it.
- Post safety looks for deeper threats.
- **KEY:** Man handles curl/flat cleanly.

#### vs ZEUS (Cover 0)
- CB on curl, FS/D on #2 flat. DL must pressure before QB finds the window.

#### vs VIKING (Cover 3)
- **FC/BC:** Bail deep, see curl develop, drive on it from deep 1/3.
- **B/D:** Curl-flat — play flat if #2 goes out, rally to curl if #2 goes vertical.
- **KEY:** Classic Cover 3 conflict. If B/D widens to flat, curl can be open behind him.

### 14.7 SLANT / FLAT (Quick Game)

\#1 runs a quick slant. #2 runs flat/arrow. Fast-developing concept that attacks inside leverage.

#### vs NINJA (Cover 7)
- **FC/BC:** Inside leverage pre-snap. Slant comes right to you — collision and match.
- **FS/D:** #2 flat — trigger and drive.
- **KEY:** Inside leverage by corners is the perfect counter to slants.

#### vs OREGON / OKLAHOMA (Cover 1)
- CBs: man on slant. Inside leverage + physicality at the LOS crucial.
- B/D: man on #2 flat — trigger quickly.
- Post safety: read QB, rally to slant if it breaks big.

#### vs ZEUS (Cover 0)
- Man on slant, man on flat. Pressure forces quick throw.
- If CB is physical at the LOS, slant is dead.

#### vs VIKING (Cover 3)
- **FC/BC:** Bail deep — slant breaks underneath. Drive on it immediately.
- **M/W:** Hook — sit on the slant as it comes across. Wall it.
- **B/D:** Curl-flat — trigger to flat.
- **KEY:** Corners bailing deep give up the slant underneath. LBs must be alert.

### 14.8 RPO CONCEPTS

QB reads a defender post-snap. If that defender bites on the run fake, QB pulls and throws behind him (bubble, now screen, glance, pop pass).

#### vs NINJA (Cover 7)
- **B** is the key. At apex, reads run/pass. If B triggers to run, #2 screen is open behind him.
- **FS/D:** Pattern-match. If ball goes to #2 quick, react and drive.
- **KEY:** B must not over-commit to the run until he reads the mesh.

#### vs OREGON / OKLAHOMA (Cover 1)
- **B** has #2. If #2 runs a bubble/now screen, B is already on him.
- **KEY:** Man coverage is naturally sound vs RPOs. Each receiver is accounted for.

#### vs ZEUS (Cover 0)
- Man coverage accounts for every receiver. RPO is covered.
- DL pressure forces quick decision. If QB holds it, he gets hit.

#### vs VIKING (Cover 3)
- **B:** Curl-flat — must not vacate flat on run fake. Stay disciplined.
- **D:** Seam-curl-flat — same discipline on boundary side.
- **KEY:** If underneath droppers bite on run fake, quick screens behind them.

### 14.9 DOUBLE POST / POST-DIG

Two receivers run post routes (or one post + one dig). Attacks MOF with two threats, stressing single-high safeties.

#### vs NINJA (Cover 7)
- Two-high: each safety matches a post entering their half.
- **KEY:** Double posts are designed to beat single-high. Two-high handles them naturally. **Best coverage vs double post.**

#### vs OREGON / OKLAHOMA (Cover 1)
- Post safety (FS or D) sits in MOF. Two posts converge on him.
- The post he doesn't take is a potential big play.
- **KEY:** Single-high vs double posts is a losing matchup. Need pressure.

#### vs ZEUS (Cover 0)
- Man on both receivers. No deep help. Both posts running free if defenders trail.
- **KEY:** Extremely vulnerable. Must get pressure before routes develop.

#### vs VIKING (Cover 3)
- **FS:** Deep middle 1/3 — splits both posts, takes whichever one QB targets.
- **FC/BC:** Deep 1/3 — lean inside to help with post entering their third.
- **KEY:** Deep middle 1/3 player splits both posts. Better than single-high Cover 1.

### 14.10 STICK (Quick Game)

\#1 runs a stick route (6 yds, settle in zone window). #2 runs a flat. #3 or RB pushes vertical as the alert. A 3-level quick concept.

#### vs NINJA (Cover 7)
- **B:** Reads #2 flat, walls it. If #3 pushes vertical, alerts safety.
- **M/W:** Hook zone — sit on the stick route. Don't let #1 settle clean.
- **KEY:** Zone drops cover all three levels.

#### vs OREGON / OKLAHOMA (Cover 1)
- **CB:** Man on #1 stick — sit on it at 6 yards.
- **B/D:** Man on #2 flat. Rally quickly.
- Post safety: read QB, help on the vertical push if it comes.

#### vs ZEUS (Cover 0)
- Man on stick, man on flat, man on vertical. All accounted for.
- Need pressure before QB reads the 3-level progression.

#### vs VIKING (Cover 3)
- **M/W:** Hook — sit on the stick.
- **B/D:** Curl-flat — drive to the flat quickly.
- Deep 1/3: stay over the top of the vertical push.
- **KEY:** Similar to curl/flat — flat defender has a conflict between sitting on stick and driving to flat.

## 15. CONCEPT vs COVERAGE QUICK REFERENCE

Which coverages are strongest and weakest against each common concept.

| Concept | NINJA (Cov 7) | Cover 1 (OR/OK) | Cover 0 (Zeus) | VIKING (Cov 3) |
|---------|---------------|-----------------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Four Verts** | **Strong** — two-high caps seams | Caution — single post vs 4 seams | Vulnerable — no help deep | **Strong** — 3 deep zones cap it |
| **Mesh / Crossers** | **Strong** — zone handles picks | Caution — picks trouble man | Vulnerable — picks + no help | **Strong** — zone handles picks |
| **Smash** | **Strong** — FS on corner, CB on hitch | **Strong** — man matches both | Caution — no help on corner | **Strong** — deep 1/3 caps corner |
| **Flood / Sail** | Caution — 3rd level stress | Caution — post must commit | **Strong** — man runs with each | Caution — flat vulnerable |
| **Levels** | **Strong** — zone reads hi-lo | Caution — long chases across field | Vulnerable — trail + no help | **Strong** — zone layers |
| **Curl / Flat** | **Strong** — two-high covers both | **Strong** — man on each | **Strong** — man on each | Caution — flat conflict |
| **Slant / Flat** | **Strong** — inside leverage + FS | **Strong** — CB physical on slant | **Strong** — pressure + man | Caution — corners bail |
| **RPO** | Caution — B must read, not bite | **Strong** — man accounts all | **Strong** — man accounts all | Caution — flat discipline |
| **Double Post** | **Strong** — two-high splits posts | Vulnerable — single post vs two | Vulnerable — no help deep | **Strong** — FS splits posts |
| **Stick** | **Strong** — zone covers all levels | **Strong** — man on each level | **Strong** — man on each | Caution — flat conflict |

### Game-Planning Guidance

- **Opponent loves four verts and double posts?** → Lean NINJA and VIKING. Two-high and three-deep handle MOF attacks best.
- **Opponent runs heavy mesh/crossers?** → NINJA and VIKING handle picks naturally. Avoid Zeus.
- **Opponent lives on quick game (slant/flat, curl/flat, stick)?** → Any coverage works. Man is clean. Pressure helps most.
- **Opponent runs heavy RPO?** → Man coverages (Oregon/Oklahoma/Zeus) account for every receiver naturally. In zone, B must stay disciplined.
- **Opponent runs flood/sail to stress the boundary?** → Zeus man is cleanest. NINJA and VIKING flat defenders can get out-leveraged.

- **MINT:** "M" on thigh.
- **ACE:** 1 finger up (optional chest tap).
- **JET:** Whoosh forward (hand flat).
- **SLIP:** Mime pushing a wall to the side (two hands).
- **Anchor Raven:** Point field → flap flap.
- **Edge Raven:** Point boundary → flap flap.

### Communication

Front/stunt/blitz calls are signaled — coverage is verbal. Signals are taught during install.

- **Front signal** first, then **stunt signal** (see Signals above).
- Pressures and coverages are signaled from the sideline per game-day call sheet.
- Safeties echo and communicate adjustments (POACH, MOD/CLAMP, BANJO, UNDER, BUMP).

### Tempo Default

When the offense's tempo is faster than we can signal our plays in, the defense defaults to the **tempo call** — set before each possession. Default tempo call: SHADE + NINJA (no stunt, no pressure). We can change the tempo call at any point during the game. Safeties must know the current tempo call at all times.

## 18. FORMATION RECOGNITION & ADJUSTMENT RULES

How we ID offensive formations and what adjustments trigger. Adjustments are **formation-driven, not coverage-driven** — we make the same adjustments regardless of coverage called.

### Formation Families

| Formation | Receivers | Key ID | Default Adjustments |
|-----------|-----------|--------|-------------------|
| **2x2** | 2 field, 2 boundary | Standard spread | None — base rules |
| **3x1** | 3 to one side, 1 backside | Trips | POACH check (NINJA), PUSH tag (Viking) |
| **Empty** | 5 wide, no RB | No back in backfield | RB funnel players become rushers or RAT |
| **2-Back** | 2 RB (20/21 personnel) | Two backs in backfield | 6-man box; B first insert for 7th hat |
| **TE Surface** | TE attached/Y-off | TE on LOS or offset | TE SET adjustment (all fronts except Grizzly) |
| **Bunch / Stack** | 3 receivers tight | Compressed splits | BANJO alert |
| **Nub TE** | Detached TE, no WR outside | Tight end as widest | Treat nub as #1; safety communicates |
| **Unbalanced** | Extra OL to one side | Tackles/guards shifted | Slide front one gap toward unbalanced side. Alert: "UNBALANCED [direction]!" May need diagram refinement. |
| **Quads (4x1)** | 4 receivers to one side | Extreme overload | Treat as trips + 1. B takes #3. M or W must expand to #4. Default to NINJA or VIKING. |

### 2x2 Adjustments

Standard look. No adjustments needed.

| Coverage | Adjustment |
|----------|-----------|
| NINJA | MOD (field) / CLAMP (boundary) |
| Cover 1 | Normal man distribution |
| Cover 0 | Normal man distribution |
| Viking | Standard zone drops |

### 3x1 (Trips) Adjustments

| Coverage | Adjustment |
|----------|-----------|
| NINJA | **POACH.** Trips to field → D is poach safety. Trips to boundary → FS is poach safety. Poach player slides to 10-12 yds splitting #2/#3 on trips side. |
| Cover 1 | See Section 8 "Cover 1 vs Trips" — B takes #3 trips. Assignments vary by Oregon/Oklahoma/Ohio. |
| Cover 0 | Safeties handle #2/#3 trips side (nearest=#2, far rotates to #3). B per Z-call. See Section 9. |
| Viking | **PUSH** tag recommended. Adjust distribution to trips side. B widens to trips, D handles backside underneath. |

### Empty Adjustments

No RB in backfield — RB funnel players are free.

| Coverage | Adjustment |
|----------|-----------|
| NINJA | **3x2 empty:** Field = MOD (FC/#1, FS/#2, B/#3). Boundary = CLAMP (BC/#1, D/#2). M/W = hook zone. **2x3 empty:** FS becomes poach on boundary #3. Field = MOD with 2. |
| Cover 1 | M/W no longer funnel. Both become rushers (5-man rush) or one becomes RAT with TAMPA. Re-ID: ensure every eligible has a man. |
| Cover 0 | All 5 eligible covered by man. M becomes free rusher. Adds to pass rush. |
| Viking | Standard drops. No run threat — play pass first. All 4 underneath players expand. M/W carry vertical/crossing threats. |

### 2-Back Adjustments

| Element | Rule |
|---------|------|
| Box | 6-man default |
| 7th hat | B is first insert |
| Cover 1 RB funnel | Both backs covered: RB to M side → M takes. RB to W side → W takes. Both release → M field, W boundary. |
| Cover 0 | B/W conditional on both RBs per normal Z-family rules |

### Bunch / Stack / Tight Splits

| Element | Rule |
|---------|------|
| Alert | **BANJO** (automatic — not signaled in) |
| Corner | First OUT |
| Near safety | First IN |
| Opposite safety | Deeper fixer |
| Rub/pick action | **EXCHANGE** automatic — switch on contact |
| LBs | Tighten alignment. Ready for quick throws underneath. |

### Motion Adjustment Summary

| Coverage Family | Motion Rule |
|----------------|------------|
| **NINJA** | Re-check: 2x2→3x1 = "POACH! POACH!" / 3x1→2x2 = "MOD/CLAMP!" Nearest safety calls, other echoes. |
| **Cover 1 / Cover 0** | **BUMP-BUMP.** Safeties travel. Underneath does NOT. Safeties do NOT cross. |

## 22. RUN SCHEME SPECIFICS

How we fit each common run scheme. Built from base rules (Section 13). Each scheme identifies: DL responsibility, LB read/fit, edge/force, and key coaching point.

---

### 22.1 INSIDE ZONE (IZ)

**Offense intent:** Combo blocks create movement. RB reads frontside A/B gap, cuts to daylight.

| Position | Responsibility |
|----------|---------------|
| **T** | Play technique. If 3-tech: squeeze B gap, don't get reached. If 2i: close A gap. |
| **N** | Play technique. Hold gap, don't get washed. |
| **A** | Contain. Set edge. Do not get reached — force cutback inside. |
| **E** | Contain. Set edge boundary. |
| **M** | Read guard-to-tackle combo. Fit open gap field side. Downhill, don't drift. |
| **W** | Read guard-to-tackle combo. Fit open gap boundary. **Key:** Be patient — watch for cutback. |
| **B** | Field force. Contain spill from the outside. |
| **D** | Boundary force. |
| **FS/D** | Run support — fill alley on run read. |
| **Key** | IZ is won by interior DL holding gaps and LBs fitting fast. Cutback is the explosive — W must be patient. |

---

### 22.2 OUTSIDE ZONE (OZ)

**Offense intent:** Stretch the defense laterally. RB reads the edge — if it's set, cut back.

| Position | Responsibility |
|----------|---------------|
| **T/N** | Reach/stretch technique. Hold gap, don't get displaced laterally. Fight pressure. |
| **A** | **Critical.** Set hard edge. DO NOT get reached. If edge is set, RB has to cut back into help. |
| **E** | Set hard edge boundary. Same coaching point. |
| **M** | Flow with the play, shuffle and fit. Don't overpursue — stay square. |
| **W** | Shuffle with flow. Be ready for cutback — don't chase frontside. |
| **B** | Force. Attack downhill on the perimeter. Force the cut back inside. |
| **D** | Boundary force on cutback. |
| **Key** | If edges are set, OZ is dead. Ball bounces back into the box. **Pursuit angles** are critical — don't overpursue. |

---

### 22.3 POWER

**Offense intent:** Kick out the edge defender. Pull a guard through the hole. Run behind double team.

| Position | Responsibility |
|----------|---------------|
| **T/N** | Play gap. Hold double team — do not get driven off the ball. |
| **A (play-side)** | Edge player. Can be kicked. If kicked: **wrong-arm the kickout block** (spill the ball outside) OR box (squeeze inside). Follow game plan. |
| **E (backside)** | Chase. Do not let bootleg/keeper come back. |
| **M** | Read pull. Stack behind DL, fit inside-out off pull path. Be physical at the POA. |
| **W** | Read pull. Fit inside-out. If play-away: slow play A, hunt cutback. |
| **B** | Overlap/force outside the kickout. If ball spills, B makes the play. |
| **D** | Boundary force. Overlap if play comes boundary. |
| **Key** | Puller rule: Box + overlap. Edge outside, M/W inside-out off pull path. Collision the puller — don't let him get to the second level clean. |

---

### 22.4 COUNTER

**Offense intent:** Misdirection with two pullers (guard + tackle or guard + H-back). Same blocking scheme as power, but backfield action goes away first.

| Position | Responsibility |
|----------|---------------|
| **T/N** | Play gap. Backside DL: chase through B gap, don't get sealed. |
| **A (play-side)** | Same as Power — edge player, handle kickout. |
| **E (backside)** | **Critical:** Stay home. Do NOT chase the initial backfield flow. Counter boot/keep comes back to the backside. |
| **M** | Read 2nd puller (usually guard). Don't bite on initial flow. Stack and fill. |
| **W** | Slow play. Read 2nd puller. Don't chase initial flow — the play is coming back. |
| **B** | Overlap outside the kick. Same as power. |
| **Key** | Discipline. Do NOT chase initial flow. Read pullers, not backs. Backside E must stay home for the keeper. |

---

### 22.5 DRAW

**Offense intent:** Fake pass, hand off late. Exploits aggressive pass rush.

| Position | Responsibility |
|----------|---------------|
| **T/N** | On pass read: get heel depth (normal rush). When you read draw action (OL stops blocking aggressively, RB takes handoff), **squeeze back down** and close lanes. |
| **A/E** | Contain. Stay in rush lane. Don't get pulled inside — QB might keep. |
| **M** | Pass read → eyes to QB → see the give → trigger downhill fast. Don't hesitate. |
| **W** | Same as M. Read the give, trigger. |
| **B** | Force on draw. Set edge, force RB inside. |
| **Key** | DL discipline on pass rush. Rush with lane integrity — don't all collapse inside. LBs must see the give quickly. |

---

### 22.6 QB RUN / DESIGNED KEEPER (CAMP)

**Offense intent:** QB runs (zone read keep, designed QB power, QB draw).

| Position | Responsibility |
|----------|---------------|
| **A** | **QB player** (CAMP). Keep outside leverage on QB. Do not chase the RB — A has the QB. |
| **E** | Backside contain. If QB cuts back, E makes the play. |
| **T/N** | Play gap responsibility. Don't chase — hold your gap. |
| **M** | Fit off DL. If QB gives to RB, M fills the run gap. If QB keeps, rally to the ball. |
| **W** | Fit off DL. Same read. |
| **B** | Force. If QB gets outside A, B must make the play. |
| **Key** | A is THE player responsible for the QB. He must keep outside leverage and not get caught inside. Everything else plays off A's discipline. |

---

### 22.7 JET SWEEP / SPEED SWEEP

**Offense intent:** Get the ball to the perimeter fast with a jet motion receiver.

| Position | Responsibility |
|----------|---------------|
| **B** | **Primary force player** (field). Must set the edge FAST. Attack downhill — do not let the sweep turn the corner. |
| **D** | Force boundary. If jet comes back (reverse/misdirection), D is the force. |
| **A** | Squeeze inside. Don't chase — hold C gap, force the ball to bounce to B. |
| **E** | Backside: stay home for cutback or reverse. |
| **M** | Fast flow to jet side. Fill inside B's force. |
| **W** | Controlled flow. **Watch misdirection** — jet fakes that come back boundary. |
| **T/N** | Play gap. Don't get reached. Squeeze. |
| **FC** | Contain/force in condensed formations. Otherwise play coverage. |
| **Key** | B must be fast to the edge. Jet sweep is won or lost at the corner. If B sets the edge, the ball cuts back into the box. |

---

### 22.8 SPLIT ZONE

**Offense intent:** Inside zone with a TE/H-back (slicer) blocking back across the formation to kick out the backside DE. The slicer removes the backside edge player.

| Position | Assignment |
|----------|-----------|
| **E** | Key the slicer. DO NOT chase the slicer across the formation. Squeeze inside — force the ball to bounce outside to D. Wrong-arm the slicer if he reaches you. |
| **W** | Backside gap integrity. The slicer is designed to remove E, so W's gap is more vulnerable. Stay square, fill backside. |
| **D** | Overlap. If E gets sealed by the slicer, D becomes the force player. Set the edge. |
| **A** | Play-side edge. Set edge, contain. |
| **T/N** | Play gap responsibilities per front alignment. |
| **M** | Fit open gap to play side. Read DL — flow with the zone. |
| **B** | Field force. Same as IZ rules. |
| **Key** | Read the linemen, not the slicer. The slicer's job is to seal E — E must squeeze and make the ball bounce. Slice rule in 4-down: E has the slicer. In 3-down: backside OLB has the slicer. |

---

### 22.9 SCREEN DEFENSE

**Recognition keys:** OL releases downfield, QB retreats behind or to the side of pocket, receivers blocking downfield.

| Position | Assignment |
|----------|-----------|
| **DL** | If OL releases and QB retreats, yell "SCREEN!" Stop rush, get hands up in throwing lane, then redirect to the ball. |
| **LBs (M/W)** | Do NOT drop to zone on screen read. Turn and sprint to screen side. Rally to the ball. |
| **Force player (B/D)** | Attack the screen side aggressively — you are the primary tackler on WR/RB screens. |
| **Corners** | If blocking pattern develops in front of you, trigger downhill to the screen. You may be the last line. |
| **Key** | DL recognition is the trigger. "SCREEN!" call alerts the entire defense. |

---

### Run Scheme Quick Reference

| Scheme | Edge Player Key | LB Key | Critical Coaching Point |
|--------|----------------|--------|------------------------|
| **IZ** | Set edge, no reach | Fit open gap fast | W patient for cutback |
| **OZ** | Set hard edge (most critical) | Flow with play, stay square | Pursuit angles — don't overpursue |
| **Power** | Handle kickout (spill/box) | Inside-out off pull | Collision the puller |
| **Counter** | Same as power | Don't bite on initial flow | Backside E stays home |
| **Draw** | Contain, rush lane integrity | See the give, trigger fast | DL squeeze back down |
| **QB Run** | A = QB player (CAMP) | Fit off DL, rally to ball | A keeps outside leverage |
| **Jet Sweep** | B sets edge fast | Fast flow, watch misdirection | Won or lost at the corner |
| **Split Zone** | E squeezes slicer | W fills backside, D overlaps | E must NOT chase slicer across |

## 23. SITUATIONAL DEFENSE

Recommended menus for key game situations. These are **starting points** — adjust weekly based on opponent tendencies.

---

### 23.1 3RD & SHORT (1-3 Yards)

**Philosophy:** Stop the run first. Accept man coverage risk. Be physical at the POA.

| Category | Recommendations |
|----------|----------------|
| **Fronts** | GRIZZLY (primary), WIDE, SHADE |
| **Stunts** | PINCH, CRASH |
| **Pressure** | Mike, sWarM |
| **Coverage** | NINJA (safe), ZEUS (aggressive) |
| **Pairing Note** | CRASH is NOT legal with GRIZZLY. Use EYES + JACKS or WIDE/SHADE + CRASH. |
| **Key Thought** | They want to run it. Line up heavy, stuff the gaps. If they throw, it's likely quick game — inside leverage handles it. |

---

### 23.2 3RD & MEDIUM (4-6 Yards)

**Philosophy:** Disguise and pressure. Two-high shell, attack from unexpected angles.

| Category | Recommendations |
|----------|----------------|
| **Fronts** | SHADE (base), UNDER |
| **Stunts** | SLANT, ANGLE |
| **Pressure** | BooM, BoW, Hammer |
| **Coverage** | NINJA (primary), OREGON |
| **Pairing Note** | SLANT is NOT legal with SHADE (use UNDER + SLANT). ANGLE is NOT legal with UNDER (use SHADE + ANGLE). |
| **Key Thought** | Most important down in football. Give them a two-high picture, bring pressure from a different spot each time. Mix zone and man behind it. |

---

### 23.3 3RD & LONG (7+ Yards)

**Philosophy:** Rush and cover. Zone coverage to take away quick throws, pressure to collapse the pocket before deeper routes develop.

| Category | Recommendations |
|----------|----------------|
| **Fronts** | SHADE, EYES |
| **Stunts** | JACKS, SLANT |
| **Pressure** | Eat (aggressive), sWarM, BooM |
| **Coverage** | NINJA (primary), VIKING |
| **Pairing Note** | SLANT is NOT legal with SHADE (use EYES + SLANT or add UNDER to front menu). JACKS is NOT legal with WIDE. |
| **Key Thought** | They need chunk yardage. Two-high or three-deep caps the deep ball. Bring extra rushers and make QB get rid of it fast. Watch for draw/screen as the series progresses. |

---

### 23.4 RED ZONE (Inside the 20)

**Philosophy:** Compressed field shrinks the offense's options. Man coverage is stronger because fewer routes can develop. Pressure more aggressively — the field is your 12th man.

| Category | Recommendations |
|----------|----------------|
| **Fronts** | GRIZZLY (inside 10), SHADE, WIDE |
| **Stunts** | PINCH, CRASH |
| **Pressure** | Mike, Will, sWarM |
| **Coverage** | ZEUS (primary), OREGON, NINJA |
| **Pairing Note** | CRASH is NOT legal with GRIZZLY. Use GRIZZLY (no stunt) or SHADE/WIDE + CRASH. |
| **Key Thought** | No explosives — worst case is a FG attempt. Man coverage works because there's less field to cover. Back-shoulder and fade routes are the biggest threat — corners must be physical. |

---

### 23.5 GOAL LINE (Inside the 5)

**Philosophy:** Stuff the run. Account for every gap. Be more physical than them.

| Category | Recommendations |
|----------|----------------|
| **Fronts** | GRIZZLY (primary) |
| **Stunts** | PINCH, CRASH |
| **Pressure** | Eat, sWarM |
| **Coverage** | ZEUS (primary), ZORRO |
| **Pairing Note** | CRASH is NOT legal with GRIZZLY. Goal line CRASH requires a non-GRIZZLY front — unlikely at the 5, but available if needed. |
| **Key Thought** | They will try to run it. Load the box, win at the point of attack. If they throw, it's fade or quick slant — man coverage, be physical. No penalties. |

---

### 23.6 2-MINUTE DEFENSE (Opponent Driving, Clock Matters)

**Philosophy:** Protect the sidelines and the deep ball. Force the offense to the middle of the field where the clock keeps running. No explosives.

| Category | Recommendations |
|----------|----------------|
| **Fronts** | SHADE (base) |
| **Stunts** | None or SLANT (occasional) |
| **Pressure** | Conservative — Mike only. Do not give up big plays chasing sacks. |
| **Coverage** | NINJA (primary), VIKING |
| **Special Rules** | |

- **Tackle in bounds.** Force them to use timeouts. Do not push runners out of bounds.
- **Watch for screens/draws** after the first few passes. Offense will try to catch aggressive pass rush off guard.
- **Clock awareness.** Know the score, timeouts remaining, and how much field they need.
- **No penalties.** An unnecessary roughness or pass interference call can change the game.
- **Prevent look (final play):** VIKING or NINJA. Deep coverage, rally to the ball.

---

### 23.7 BACKED UP (Own 1-10 Yard Line)

**Philosophy:** Bend but don't break. Every yard matters. No explosives, no penalties. Force them to earn it.

| Category | Recommendations |
|----------|----------------|
| **Fronts** | SHADE, WIDE |
| **Stunts** | PINCH |
| **Pressure** | Conservative — Mike or Will only. Do not gamble near your own goal line. |
| **Coverage** | NINJA (primary) |
| **Special Rules** | |

- **No penalties.** A 15-yard penalty inside your own 10 is catastrophic.
- **Corners play trail technique.** Do not get beat deep for a 90-yard touchdown. Let them catch it underneath and tackle.
- **DL:** Win the LOS. Don't let them push the pile for easy yards.
- **LBs:** Stay square, fill fast. No over-pursuing — a missed tackle is a touchdown.
- **Safeties:** Stay deep. You are the last line. Do not trigger on play fakes.

---

### 23.8 4-MINUTE / PROTECT THE LEAD

**Philosophy:** Keep the clock running. Force runs inside. Don't give up chunk plays or sideline routes. Make them earn first downs.

| Category | Recommendations |
|----------|----------------|
| **Fronts** | SHADE, EYES |
| **Stunts** | Minimal — PINCH if needed |
| **Pressure** | None unless necessary. Blitzing creates risk of big plays. |
| **Coverage** | VIKING (primary), NINJA |
| **Special Rules** | |

- **Priority:** Force the ball to stay in the middle of the field. Clock keeps running.
- **Tackle in bounds** (same as 2-minute but reversed — you want the clock to run).
- **Don't give up chunk yardage.** Underneath completions are OK if the clock runs.
- **Run support first.** They may try to run to keep their own clock moving — stop it and force 3rd downs.
- **Manage risk.** If you force a punt, you win the game. Don't gamble for a turnover and give up a first down instead.

---

### Situational Quick Reference

| Situation | Primary Front | Primary Coverage | Pressure Level | Key Rule |
|-----------|--------------|-----------------|----------------|----------|
| 3rd & Short | GRIZZLY | NINJA / ZEUS | Moderate | Stop the run |
| 3rd & Medium | SHADE | NINJA / OREGON | Aggressive | Disguise + pressure |
| 3rd & Long | SHADE / EYES | NINJA / VIKING | Aggressive | Rush and cover |
| Red Zone | GRIZZLY / SHADE | ZEUS / OREGON | Aggressive | Man coverage, be physical |
| Goal Line | GRIZZLY | ZEUS / ZORRO | All-out | Stuff every gap |
| 2-Minute | SHADE | NINJA / VIKING | Conservative | Protect sidelines, tackle in bounds |
| Backed Up | SHADE / WIDE | NINJA | Conservative | No penalties, no explosives |
| 4-Minute | SHADE / EYES | VIKING / NINJA | None | Keep clock running |

### 26.1 M/W Gap Fit by Front

| Front | T Align | N Align | M Gap (Field) | W Gap (Boundary) | Notes |
|-------|---------|---------|---------------|-------------------|-------|
| **SHADE** | 3 (field) | 2i (bnd) | Open A | Open B | T closes B → M has A. N closes A → W has B. |
| **UNDER** | 2i (field) | 3 (bnd) | Open B | Open A | Opposite of SHADE. |
| **EYES** | 2i (field) | 2i (bnd) | Open B | Open B | Both interior in A gaps → both LBs in B. |
| **WIDE** | 3 (field) | 3 (bnd) | Open A | Open A | Both interior in B gaps → both LBs in A. |
| **DEUCES** | 2 (field) | 2 (bnd) | React to T | React to N | T closes A → M fills B. T stays B → M fills A. Same for W/N. |
| **GRIZZLY** | — | — | Head up C at LB depth (no TE); head up G to TE side at LB depth (TE); C gap | OLB | B & W are OLBs. TE SET does NOT override. |
| **BOSS** | 3 (field) | 1 (field) | A (boundary) | B (boundary) | LBs fit opposite DL strength. |
| **BOSS UNDER** | 1 (bnd) | 3 (bnd) | A (field) | B (field) | LBs fit opposite DL strength. |

**Key Reads for M/W:**
- **3-tech side:** 3-tech closes B → open gap is A.
- **2i/1-tech side:** closes A → open gap is B.
- **2-tech (DEUCES):** read the DL — react to which gap he takes.
- **Stunt changes it:** If a stunt moves DL into a different gap, LB fills the vacated gap.

### 26.2 NINJA in 3 Sentences (Day 1 Teaching)

> **"NINJA is split-field zone coverage. Each side of the field has its own rules based on how many receivers are to that side. Corners play #1, safeties play #2, and Bandit is the swing player in the middle."**

**Day 1 Teach Progression:**
1. **Alignment:** Corners inside leverage at 6. Safeties at 10-12 on #2. B at apex.
2. **2x2 rule:** Field side = MOD (FC + FS + B). Boundary side = CLAMP (BC + D).
3. **3x1 rule:** Trips side gets MOD. Poach safety slides from backside to help.
4. **Motion:** If motion creates or removes trips, re-check (yell "POACH" or "MOD/CLAMP").
5. **Special:** Bunch/stack = BANJO alert. Rubs = EXCHANGE.

## 27. OPTION DEFENSE APPENDIX

### Philosophy
"Assign every option phase to a specific defender. No one freelances. Everyone has a job — do your job and trust your teammates to do theirs."

### Base Option Assignments (4-Down)

| Phase | Defender | Technique |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| **Dive** | T and N (A/B gaps) | Attack downhill. Spill or stuff. Make the QB pull. |
| **QB** | A (CAMP rule) | Slow play. Do NOT chase the dive. Force the pitch. |
| **Pitch** | B (field) / D (boundary) | Force player. Attack the pitch man. Turn everything inside. |
| **Cutback** | M and W | Fill cutback lanes inside-out. Be patient — don't over-pursue. |

### Front Adjustments for Option

| Front | Best vs Option? | Notes |
|-------|----------------|-------|
| SHADE | YES | Clear dive/QB/pitch assignments. Base option front. |
| UNDER | YES | Works if option runs to boundary (N is 3-tech to bnd). |
| EYES | OK | Both DTs in A gap — dive is accounted for. QB player slightly less clear. |
| WIDE | NO | Both DTs in B gap — A gaps exposed to dive. Avoid vs heavy option teams. |
| GRIZZLY | OK | 4i alignment helps vs inside option. M at 10-tech can be option QB player to TE side. |

### Option vs 3-Down Packages

| Package | Dive | QB | Pitch |
|---------|------|----|-------|
| MINT | T (0-tech) | B (QB player in man) or M (NINJA/VIKING) | Force player (B field / D boundary) |
| ACE | A and N (edges) | B | Force player |
| JET | T (0-tech) | A/N (contain edges) | Force player |
| SLIP | T (0-tech) | A | Force player |

### Flexbone / Triple-Option Specifics
- **Dive read:** DL must attack their gap and NOT follow the mesh. T/N play A/B gaps aggressively.
- **QB read:** A slow-plays the QB. Do NOT commit to dive. Wait for the QB to declare, then attack.
- **Pitch read:** Force player must get outside leverage on the pitch man BEFORE the ball arrives. If you're late, wrong-arm and force it back inside.
- **Motion/Rocket:** Treat jet motion as pitch phase. Force player widens to account for speed option.
- **Midline (A-gap option):** N is the dive player. A has QB. This is interior option — LBs must be inside-out.

### Option Defense Coaching Points
1. **No one has two phases.** Every defender is assigned ONE job. If someone tries to play two phases, someone is free.
2. **Slow play > fast play.** Patience at the point of attack prevents the offense from reading you. Make them hold the ball.
3. **Tackle downhill.** Option teams want you running east-west. Attack north-south, take ground, and tackle.
4. **Play your assignment even when it hurts.** If your phase doesn't get the ball, you did your job. Trust teammates.
5. **Film study matters.** Know their tendencies — what down/distance/hash do they run each option phase?
