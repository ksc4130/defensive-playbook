## Agenda

### 1. Defensive Identity (15 min)

**Key message:** This is who we are. Everything we teach traces back to this.

**Cover:**
- Philosophy statement: "We play fast, physical, and disciplined football. Our job is to remove explosive plays, control the box, and force the offense to execute perfectly for an entire drive."
- Base structure at a glance:
  - 4-2-5 personnel, 3-4 packages available
  - Field strength ("protect space")
  - Two-high shell pre-snap
  - 6-man box default
- The 8 non-negotiables — read through all 8, brief discussion on each
- The Standard — six standards, no exceptions

**Talking points:**
- We are a multiple defense with simple rules. Lots of looks, not lots of learning.
- Two-high shell is our disguise. What we do after the snap changes — the picture before it stays consistent.
- "No explosives" is non-negotiable #1 for a reason. Everything else supports it.

---

### 2. Position Designations — Our Language (10 min)

**Key message:** Every coach needs to speak the same language starting today.

**Cover:**
- The 11 position letters: FC, FS, B, A, T, N, E, M, W, D, BC
- Default sides: A/T = field, N/E = boundary
- What "field" and "boundary" mean for us (strength to the wide side)
- Contain vs. force — brief distinction

**Talking points:**
- Letters, not traditional names. "Anchor" not "field DE." "Bandit" not "nickel." This keeps communication clean.
- B (Bandit) is the hybrid — he's a DB in coverage, an OLB in 3-down. He's the most versatile player on the field.
- Safeties are the fixers. FS owns field-side communication. D owns boundary.

---

### 3. Call Grammar (10 min)

**Key message:** Every call follows the same formula. Once you know the formula, you can read any call.

**Cover:**
- Formula: **Front + Stunt + Blitz + Coverage/Tags**
- Walk through 3-4 examples on the board:
  - `SHADE + NINJA` (base — simplest call)
  - `UNDER + SLANT + ZEUS` (front + stunt + coverage)
  - `GRIZZLY + sWarM + ZORRO` (short yardage front + combo pressure + zero coverage)
  - `EYES + JACKS + sWarM + ZORRO` (full call with all four pieces)
- Communication: front/stunt/blitz signaled — coverage is verbal. Safeties echo and relay.
- Tempo call concept: when the offense's tempo is faster than we can signal our plays in, we default to a pre-set call (default: SHADE + NINJA)

**Talking points:**
- The call tells every player exactly what to do. No ambiguity.
- Coaches need to know the grammar so they can help players decode calls in real time.
- Tempo call is our safety net.

---

### 4. The System Overview — What's in the Toolbox (15 min)

**Key message:** The system is built. Here's what's in it. We'll install it piece by piece.

**Cover the MENU, not the details:**

**Fronts (names and 1-line purpose only):**
- SHADE — base front, what we live in
- UNDER — boundary tendency breaker
- EYES — balanced, good vs zone/duo
- WIDE — force the bounce
- DEUCES — Wing-T / puller defense
- GRIZZLY — goal line, power/counter/zone, edge pressure
- BOSS / BOSS UNDER — overload looks, pressure setups

**3-Down Packages (concept only):**
- MINT, ACE, JET, SLIP — triggered by offensive personnel
- Same coverage system, different front structure

**Stunts (names only):**
- SLANT, ANGLE, PINCH, JACKS, SPLIT, CRASH, VEER, ANCHOR/EDGE ATTACK, RAVENS
- "We have movement. It's organized. DL coaches — this is your world."

**Pressures (names only):**
- Singles, combos, packaged pressures
- Weekly menu is locked by Friday
- "We mostly pressure with a plus-one rusher advantage, putting the offense in a no-win situation with zero coverage behind it."

**Coverages — the four families:**
- NINJA (Cover 7) — pattern-match quarters. Our base. Most of our snaps.
- Cover 1 (Oregon / Oklahoma / Ohio) — man-match, MOF safety variations
- Cover 0 / Z-Family (Zeus, Zorro, Zunnel, Zill, Zike) — zero, run-first pressure
- VIKING (Cover 3) — spot-drop, safe, anti-explosive call-off

**Talking points:**
- 8 fronts, 4 packages, 11 stunts, 12+ pressures, 4 coverage families. That sounds like a lot — it's not. The rules are simple and consistent. The LOOKS are multiple.
- We don't install all of this at once. Camp is 10 days. It's layered.
- NINJA is the coverage we'll spend the most time on. It's the engine of the defense.
- Zeus is our run-stopper that doubles as delayed pressure. Show it, then call it off to NINJA or VIKING.

---

### 5. How It All Fits Together (5 min)

**Key message:** Front controls the box. Coverage controls the shell. Stunts and pressures create chaos within structure.

**Cover:**
- Draw a base SHADE + NINJA look on the board — label all 11 positions
- Point out: two-high shell, 6-man box, field strength
- Then draw SHADE + NINJA with ANGLE stunt — same shell, different DL movement
- Then draw SHADE + Zeus — same front, different coverage intent (run-first)
- "Same picture pre-snap. Different answers post-snap. That's the whole idea."
